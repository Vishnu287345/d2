INTRODUCTION

Food refers to anything that is eaten to provide energy and keep the body healthy. It forms an important part of many Ghanaian customs and traditions. Whilst some foods are not regarded as food at all by some groups, the same foods are delicacies for others. As a caterer you need to learn about the role food plays in the life of the different people you have to cater for. People need food especially because it keeps them healthy and offer enjoyment. If the food you offer for sale does not do any of the above people may not patronize your services. This is why it is important for you to learn how to make wise food choices and provide the kinds of food your varied customers want.

ABSTRACT

This community service project aims to analyze the food habits of local residents. The survey collects data on dietary preferences, frequency of meals, and consumption of various food groups. It also examines factors influencing food choices such as cultural background, health awareness, and economic status. The goal is to identify patterns and suggest areas for nutritional improvement.

EXISTING BEFORE SURVEY

1) Limited Nutritional Knowledge
Situation:

Many members lacked basic understanding of nutrition, including the importance of balanced diets, reading food labels, and understanding portion sizes.

Impact:

Poor dietary choices and health issues such as obesity and diabetes.

2) High Reliance on Processed Foods
Situation:

Due to busy lifestyles and convenience, many individuals relied heavily on fast food and processed foods which are high in sugars, unhealthy fats and sodium.

Impact:

Increased the risk of chronic diseases.

3) Cultural and Social Influences
Situation:

Cultural traditions often included high-calorie, low-nutrient foods with little emphasis on healthy eating.

Impact:

Persistent unhealthy eating patterns across generations.

4) Lack of Community Resources
Situation:

There were few programs promoting nutrition education or providing access to healthy foods.

Impact:

Limited support to individuals seeking to improve their diets.

PROPOSED AFTER SURVEY

1) Enhanced Nutritional Knowledge
Change:

We have taught people to eat balanced diet foods and to make healthier food choices like vegetables, fruits which are rich in vitamins and minerals

Impact:

Improved understanding of nutrition led to better dietary decisions and increased awareness of healthy eating benefits.

2) Shift towards Fresh and Whole Foods
Change:

We have given some suggestions to take initiatives such as to setup community gardens(a piece of land where people can grow flowers, vegetables individually or collectively) and mobile markets(a digital marketing strategy that uses mobile devices to promote products and services to customers)

Impact:

The partnerships with local farmers also help them to increase access to fresh produce.

3) Culturally Relevant Education
Change:

We have given an idea to conduct nutrition education programs and to include cultural food practices, offering healthier alternatives within the system of rules.

Impact:

Improved acceptance and implementation of healthy eating habits within diverse cultural contexts.

CONCLUSION

The suggestions given during the project guided people to make healthier dietary decisions. We concluded that healthier food choices positively impacted the community. By learning from traditional food habits and integrating healthier alternatives, the community is more aware of the benefits of a nutritious diet. Remember, "Eat good, feel good".
